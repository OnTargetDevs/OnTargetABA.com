About Optum behavioral health

Why your card says UHC but the auth comes from Optum.

UnitedHealth Group owns both UnitedHealthcare (the insurance plan) and Optum (the behavioral-health network manager). When you have a UHC plan, Optum is who actually reviews the ABA treatment plan, issues the authorization, and renews it every six months.

That’s good news for you. Optum has a single national medical policy for ABA, so a Choice Plus plan in Utah and an Oxford plan in New York follow the same rules. Once we’re credentialed (we are), the path is consistent. Our BCBAs write Optum-compliant treatment plans, submit re-auths on time, and respond to peer reviews so therapy never lapses.

  • Optum requires a current autism diagnosis from a qualified provider (we can refer you if you need testing)
  • Initial authorization is typically 6 months, then renews based on progress data
  • No annual session caps on most UHC commercial plans
  • Medical-necessity reviews use the BCBA’s assessment, not a flat formula

Questions families ask us

UnitedHealthcare & ABA, answered honestly.

Does every UnitedHealthcare plan cover ABA therapy?
Nearly every commercial UHC plan covers ABA as a behavioral-health benefit when there’s a documented autism diagnosis. The exceptions are some short-term gap plans and a handful of self-funded employer plans that have explicitly carved out ABA. We verify your specific plan in 24 hours so you don’t have to guess.

What will I actually pay out of pocket?
For most families on UHC, the answer is $0 once the family deductible is met — and because ABA is delivered 15–40 hours a week, most families hit that deductible inside the first month or two. Plans with copays usually run $20–$40 per session day. Surest plans show your copay up front. We tell you the exact number in writing before you start.

My card says UMR, Surest, or Optum. Is that still UHC?
Yes. UMR, Surest, Oxford, and Empire are all UnitedHealth Group brands, and Optum is the behavioral-health arm that reviews ABA authorizations. We’re in network across all of them. Send us the card and we’ll confirm which network your specific plan uses.

How long does prior authorization take?
Optum’s standard turnaround on a new ABA authorization is 7–14 business days after we submit the assessment. We schedule the BCBA assessment within a week of intake, so the typical timeline from first phone call to first therapy session is 3–5 weeks. Renewals run on a 6-month cycle.

What if my child doesn’t have an autism diagnosis yet?
UnitedHealthcare covers diagnostic evaluations as a medical benefit, and we provide testing in-house with no waitlist — usually scheduled within a week. If your child qualifies for an ABA recommendation, we can roll directly into therapy with the same team. One front door, no handoffs.

Autism Scholarship & Funding Programs

We're an approved provider for state autism scholarship programs and we help families navigate Medicaid waivers and private grants. If you're worried about cost, start here — most families pay $0 out of pocket once a program is in place.

Talk to us →
OH

Ohio families

  • Ohio Autism Scholarship Program (ASP)
    Up to $32,455/year for ABA, autism evaluation, speech, OT, and related services. Administered by the Ohio Department of Education and Workforce. On Target ABA is an approved ASP provider and bills the state directly.
  • Jon Peterson Special Needs Scholarship
    Alternative funding for students on an IEP with disabilities other than (or in addition to) autism.
  • Ohio Medicaid & managed-care plans
    Buckeye, Molina, CareSource, Anthem, AmeriHealth Caritas, and Humana Healthy Horizons all cover ABA for children with an autism diagnosis.
UT

Utah families

  • Utah Medicaid (ABA benefit)
    Covers medically necessary ABA for children with an autism diagnosis, including center-based and in-home hours.
  • Medicaid Autism Waiver (DSPD)
    Through the Division of Services for People with Disabilities — waitlist-based; we can help families understand eligibility and apply.
  • Carson Smith Special Needs Scholarship
    State scholarship for K–12 students with disabilities; can offset costs for related services alongside private insurance.
  • Private grants
    UnitedHealthcare Children's Foundation, ACT Today, and the Autism Care Today Family Grant accept rolling applications nationwide.

Eligibility at a glance

  • • A current autism diagnosis from a qualified provider (we offer evaluations if you don't have one yet)
  • • Child between roughly age 2 and 21 (varies by program)
  • • Residency in the program's state — Ohio or Utah for the scholarships above
  • • Most ASP and Medicaid programs are not income-restricted

How to apply (we help with every step)

  1. Call us — we'll confirm which program your child qualifies for.
  2. Gather the diagnostic report, IEP (if applicable), and proof of residency.
  3. We submit the provider paperwork and you submit the family application.
  4. Once approved, we coordinate hours, scheduling, and billing directly.

Insurance FAQ

Quick answers about coverage.

We accept most insurances. Generally, insurance companies are required by law to cover ABA therapy for children with autism diagnoses, however there are some exceptions. If you are not sure if your insurance covers ABA therapy give us a call and one of our insurance experts can verify if you are covered.

Yes. We accept Medicaid plans.

If your plan isn't listed, don't worry. We routinely work with families whose carriers aren't in our standard network. Contact us and we'll verify your benefits, explore single-case agreements, and walk you through any out-of-pocket considerations before treatment begins.

You'll typically need (1) an autism diagnosis report, (2) a doctor's recommendation for ABA therapy if your insurance requires one, and (3) your insurance information so we can verify coverage. Most insurances are required to cover ABA therapy — we'll help you check.

We move quickly. After we collect your information, we coordinate and communicate with your insurance company on your behalf to obtain approval for requested services. Most families can start ABA therapy as soon as your insurance sends us their approval.

Cost & coverage

What does ABA therapy cost?

For most families we work with, ABA therapy is covered by insurance or Medicaid — meaning $0 typical out-of-pocket beyond standard copays and deductibles. Below are ballpark ranges for self-pay families and a few common scenarios. For exact pricing on your plan, please call us — rates and benefits change frequently.

With in-network insurance
$0

Self-pay / uninsured

Up to $32k/yr

What's included in the rate

  • • 1:1 direct therapy hours with a Registered Behavior Technician (RBT)
  • • BCBA supervision, program design, and parent training
  • • Initial assessment and treatment plan
  • • Progress reports and insurance re-authorization paperwork

If cost is a concern

  • • Payment plans available for self-pay families

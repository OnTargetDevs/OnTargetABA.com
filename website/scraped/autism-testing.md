## When should you consider Autism Testing?

Many Salt Lake Valley parents contact us after noticing:

- Delayed Speech
- Limited eye contact
- Not responding to name
- Repetitive behaviors
- Sensory sensitivities
- Social interaction difficulties

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, early identification of autism spectrum disorder allows children to access support sooner.

Getting an autism evaluation does not label your child. It provides clarity and direction.

## What happens during an Autism Evaluation?

Our autism testing process for Salt Lake Valley families includes:

- **Parent Interview** — We discuss developmental history and concerns in detail.
- **Standardized Autism Assessment** — The licensed psychologist uses validated tools to assess communication and behavioral patterns.
- **Structured Observation** — Carefully guided interaction allows for accurate developmental evaluation.
- **Written Diagnostic Report** — You receive a clear, detailed autism diagnosis report if criteria are met.
- **Clear Next Steps** — We explain recommended services and how to move forward.

## "If my child is diagnosed, what happens next?"

If your child is diagnosed with autism, early intervention services such as ABA therapy are highly recommended.

At On Target ABA in Salt Lake City, we provide:

- Center-based ABA therapy
- Parent education

This means you do not have to start over or search for another provider. We help coordinate next steps so your child can begin receiving ABA therapy as soon as possible.

Having autism testing and ABA therapy under one roof allows for smoother transitions and faster care.

## Why early Autism Diagnosis matters

Early diagnosis of autism spectrum disorder can:

- Open access to insurance-covered therapies
- Allow earlier intervention services
- Support school planning and IEP eligibility
- Improve long-term developmental outcomes

The earlier children receive appropriate support, the better the outcomes tend to be.

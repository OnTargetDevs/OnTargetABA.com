### Q: What does a typical ABA session look like at On Target ABA's Murray center?

Sessions at On Target ABA Therapy in Murray, Utah, are structured, supportive, and engaging. While each child's program is individualized, most sessions combine play-based learning with evidence-based behavioral strategies.

Session components:

- Warm, child-friendly environment with multiple therapy rooms
- Trained behavior technician paired with child
- Communication practice, social skills, functional play
- Independent play and daily living skills activities
- Naturalistic, relationship-focused teaching approach
- Targeted support for transitions, new situations, emotional regulation
- Every activity tied to individualized treatment plan
- Built-in social opportunities
- Support for generalization into home, school, community

At On Target ABA, every session is designed to help your child build confidence, independence, and meaningful life skills — all in a supportive local setting right here in Murray.

### Q: How will I know if ABA therapy at On Target ABA is working?

One of the biggest strengths of ABA therapy, especially at On Target ABA in Murray, is that progress is always measured with clear, objective data. You'll never be left wondering whether therapy is helping.

Key points:

- Detailed assessment and individualized plan with measurable goals
- Data collected during every session
- Continuous review and adjustments
- Regular parent updates, monthly meetings, progress summaries, graphs
- Data-driven approach shows how and why improvements happen

**Progress at On Target ABA often looks like:**

- more consistent communication
- better play and social engagement
- improved independence in daily routines
- fewer behavioral challenges
- increased confidence and flexibility

### Q: What happens if my child struggles during ABA therapy?

It's completely normal for children to experience frustration or pushback at times during therapy — and On Target ABA is experienced in helping children work through those challenges in a supportive way.

Approach:

- BCBA carefully reviews situation and adjusts teaching
- Not "one-size-fits-all" strategies
- May switch to naturalistic methods, increase reinforcement, shorten tasks
- Incorporate child's interests
- Use sensory room breaks when needed
- Parents receive guidance for home implementation

Struggles never mean therapy isn't working — they often signal that your child is learning new skills or needs a different teaching approach.

### Q: How much parent involvement is required in ABA therapy?

At On Target ABA in Murray, parents are essential partners in the therapy process. While you won't need to be present during every session (especially center-based therapy), your involvement helps ensure your child's progress carries over into real life.

Parent involvement includes:

- Consistent parent training
- Monthly or biweekly meetings
- Practicing communication skills at home
- Encouraging independence in daily living
- Supporting routines and transitions
- Reinforcing positive behaviors

You don't need special experience or training to participate — On Target ABA teaches everything step-by-step.

### Q: Is ABA therapy at On Target ABA only for young children?

While early intervention is extremely beneficial, ABA therapy at On Target ABA is not limited to toddlers or preschoolers. Many children in elementary, middle, and even high school receive ABA services to build independence, communication, life skills, and social functioning.

Older children may work on:

- emotional regulation
- peer and social relationships
- daily living routines
- school readiness and classroom behavior
- community safety
- coping skills

### Q: How long will my child need ABA therapy?

The duration of ABA therapy varies widely depending on your child's needs, goals, and progress. At On Target ABA, therapy plans are not open-ended. They develop a personalized timeline based on assessment results and your family's goals.

Details:

- Intensive therapy may be 20–40 hours per week for several years
- Part-time programs or short-term services for specific goals
- Therapy hours often decrease as children gain independence
- Regular progress review and program adjustments

The ultimate goal is improved functioning, strong communication, increased independence, and successful participation in school and community life in Murray.

### Q: How do I choose the right ABA provider in Murray, Utah?

Questions to ask any provider:

- Is a BCBA supervising my child's therapy regularly?
- Is data collected and reviewed often?
- Will I receive parent training?
- What does the center environment look like?
- Do therapists build relationships with the children they serve?

On Target ABA encourages families to tour the Murray center, meet the team, and understand their approach before starting. This transparency helps you feel confident in your decision.

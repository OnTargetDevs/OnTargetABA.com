The price tag, in plain English

With insurance, most families pay nothing.

Autism evaluations are considered a medically necessary diagnostic service. That means they’re covered by most private insurance plans and by Medicaid — usually with no copay, no deductible to chip through, and no surprise bill at the end.

Before we schedule anything, we run a benefits verification with your carrier. You see the numbers in writing — in your name, on your plan — before you book.

Commercial insurance

Aetna, Anthem, Cigna, United, BCBS, Select Health and more typically cover the evaluation at 100% once any deductible is met — and for most diagnostic codes, no deductible applies.

Medicaid & CHIP

Utah Medicaid and most state Medicaid managed-care plans cover the evaluation in full. No copay. No paperwork shuffle. We bill directly.

Self-pay

If you don’t have coverage or prefer not to bill insurance, the full self-pay evaluation typically runs $2,000–$4,000 depending on testing depth. We’ll quote you a fixed number upfront — no surprises.

What you’re actually paying for

A real evaluation, not a checkbox visit.

Every On Target ABA evaluation is led by a licensed clinician and includes the assessments, observations, scoring, family interview, and a written diagnostic report you can hand to a school, pediatrician, or therapy provider.

Parent interview

A structured developmental history covering pregnancy, milestones, sleep, sensory patterns, language, and social play.

Standardized testing

Gold-standard tools such as the ADOS-2 plus age-matched developmental and cognitive measures.

Direct observation

Play-based and structured tasks so the clinician sees your child in real-world interactions, not just on a form.

Written report

A full diagnostic write-up with scores, clinical impressions, and clear next-step recommendations — usually within two weeks.

Cost questions, answered

The questions parents actually ask.

Is the autism evaluation really free with insurance?
For most of the families we work with, yes — $0 out of pocket. Autism diagnostic evaluations are categorized as medically necessary, and the major commercial plans plus Medicaid cover them at 100%. The only common exception is a small copay if your plan treats the visit as a specialist appointment. We tell you the exact figure before you book.

What does $2,000 to $4,000 actually buy you on self-pay?
The full diagnostic package: parent interview, standardized tools like the ADOS-2, developmental and cognitive screening, direct observation, scoring, clinician review, and a written report you can use with schools, pediatricians, and therapy providers. Where you land in that $2,000–$4,000 band depends on your child’s age and the depth of testing the clinician recommends.

Will I owe anything if my insurance denies the claim?
We run the benefits verification before you book, so denials are rare. If one does happen, we appeal it on your behalf and keep you in the loop. You won’t receive a surprise bill — any out-of-pocket responsibility is documented and confirmed with you before services are rendered.

Do you accept Utah Medicaid?
Yes. Utah Medicaid and most major managed-care Medicaid plans are in-network for autism evaluations through On Target ABA. There is typically no copay, no deductible, and no balance billing.

How quickly can we get scheduled?
Most families are on the calendar within one week of the benefits check — not six months, not eighteen months. If a diagnosis confirms autism, our team can begin ABA therapy without a second referral or new waitlist.

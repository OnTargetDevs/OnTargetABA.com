What you’re looking at

This is the current homepage headline rendered with the active toggles. Switch “Display font” to see the chunkier Bricolage Grotesque applied to the H1, the stat figures, and the eyebrow numerals. Switch “Hero visual” to swap the target-mark roundel for the new yellow-sun device with child photo.

Tradeoff — font: Fraunces feels editorial and clinical-trustworthy; Bricolage feels modern, tactile, and closer to the Figma board. Body type stays Plus Jakarta Sans in both cases so paragraph rhythm is identical.

Tradeoff — hero: The target mark is brand-distinctive and works without photography. The yellow-sun device leads with a child’s face — warmer, more emotional, stronger on real outcomes. It also needs a curated photo per page.

What you’re looking at

A second hero treatment for the autism-testing page, with the visual moved to the left and three stat cards anchoring the right column. This is the layout where the yellow-sun device shines — the child’s face directly answers the question “will my kid be okay here?” faster than any roundel can.

Notice how the stat numerals (0, 2–3, 7d) pick up the active display font. With Bricolage the figures feel chunkier and more poster-like; with Fraunces they feel editorial.

Decision criteria: if the brand wants to lean into “warm, modern, family-first,” Bricolage + yellow-sun is the move. If it wants to stay closer to the medical/clinical lane it’s in today, Fraunces + target-mark stays.

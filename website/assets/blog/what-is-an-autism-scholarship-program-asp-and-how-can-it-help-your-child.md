# What Is an Autism Scholarship Program (ASP) and How Can It Help Your Child?

If you're a parent of a child with autism in Ohio, you may have heard the term ASP — the Autism Scholarship Program.

However, many families aren't quite sure what it actually means.

Is it financial aid?

Is it insurance?

Is it a therapy program?

The [Autism Scholarship Program Ohio](https://education.ohio.gov/Topics/Other-Resources/Scholarships/Autism-Scholarship) is a state-funded scholarship that gives parents the choice to send their child to a special education provider outside of their public school district — while still receiving the services outlined in their child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) or Autism Education Plan (AEP).

In simple terms:

It gives families options.

And options matter.

## What Is the Autism Scholarship Program Ohio?

The Autism Scholarship Program Ohio was created to give parents flexibility in choosing the educational setting that best supports their child's needs. Instead of being limited to the special education program operated by their district of residence, eligible families may choose to enroll with a participating scholarship provider.

The state then directs scholarship funds to the approved provider to deliver the services outlined in the child's IEP or AEP.

This may include:

* ABA therapy
* Speech therapy
* Occupational therapy
* Specialized instruction
* Behavioral support services

The goal is not to replace public education — it is to give families the ability to choose what works best for their child.

## Who Qualifies for the Autism Scholarship Program Ohio?

Eligibility for the Autism Scholarship Program Ohio requires that a student meet at least one of the following criteria:

1. The student has been identified by their district as a child with autism and has a finalized IEP that reflects that diagnosis.

OR

2. The student receives a private diagnosis of autism and that diagnosis is reflected in their IEP or Autism Education Plan (AEP) developed by the district of residence.

A child becomes eligible to apply when they turn three years old.

That early eligibility is important. It allows families to access structured services during critical developmental years.

## How the Autism Scholarship Program Ohio Works

Once a child is deemed eligible, the parent or guardian must enroll with a participating scholarship provider.

If the family enrolls with a business provider (rather than a chartered nonpublic school), the parent assumes responsibility for home educating their child.

That structure ensures that the educational and therapeutic services align with the child's individualized plan.

The Autism Scholarship Program Ohio funds are then directed to the provider to cover approved services.

The amount awarded is determined by the state and may vary based on services needed.

## Private Diagnosis and the Autism Scholarship Program Ohio

For families pursuing eligibility through a private diagnosis, certain requirements must be met.

A valid private diagnosis should:

* Be dated and signed by a licensed doctor or psychologist
* Include the provider's address and telephone number
* Clearly identify the student (name, address, date of birth)
* Clearly state the autism diagnosis

This documentation must then be reflected in the child's IEP or AEP developed by the district of residence.

While this process may feel administrative, it ensures that services are aligned with formal documentation.

Clarity protects both families and providers.

## Why the Autism Scholarship Program Ohio Matters

Raising a child with autism often means navigating complex systems.

School meetings. Therapy appointments. [Insurance questions](https://ontargetaba.com/insurance/). Evaluations.

The Autism Scholarship Program Ohio helps simplify one major concern: educational placement.

Instead of feeling confined to one district-operated option, families can choose a participating provider that best meets their child's needs.

For some children, that means:

* Smaller, structured settings
* Intensive ABA-based programs
* Individualized behavioral support
* More targeted skill development

Choice creates opportunity.

And opportunity creates progress.

## Common Questions Parents Ask

### Does the scholarship replace my child's IEP?

No. The IEP or AEP remains the guiding document for services. The scholarship allows those services to be delivered through a participating provider instead of the district program.

### Can my child receive ABA therapy through the scholarship?

Yes — if ABA therapy is outlined in the IEP or AEP and delivered by an approved provider, it may be covered under the Autism Scholarship Program Ohio.

### Is income a factor?

The Autism Scholarship Program Ohio is not income-based. Eligibility is based on diagnosis and IEP/AEP documentation.

## The Emotional Side of Choosing ASP

Choosing a different educational pathway can feel overwhelming.

You may wonder:

Am I making the right choice?
Will this be better for my child?
What if I regret it?

These questions are normal.

However, the Autism Scholarship Program Ohio exists because one size does not fit all.

Some children thrive in traditional district settings. Others benefit from more intensive or specialized services.

Having the ability to choose gives parents a stronger voice in their child's educational journey.

And that empowerment matters.

## What Happens After Enrollment?

Once enrolled with a participating provider, services begin according to the child's IEP or AEP.

Progress is typically monitored through:

* Data collection
* Regular progress updates
* Parent communication
* IEP/AEP review meetings

The structure remains individualized.

The difference is the environment.

## Final Thoughts

The Autism Scholarship Program Ohio is about flexibility, empowerment, and access.

It gives parents of children with autism the opportunity to choose a special education provider that aligns with their child's unique needs.

For eligible families, it can open doors to ABA therapy, specialized instruction, and targeted behavioral support — all funded through state scholarship dollars.

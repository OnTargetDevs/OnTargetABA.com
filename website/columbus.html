Our approach

What ABA actually looks like in our Columbus rooms.

Your child is paired 1:1 with a Registered Behavior Technician who knows their goals, their reinforcers and their routine. A Board Certified Behavior Analyst oversees the plan and reviews progress weekly. Sessions feel like play — because for kids, that’s how learning sticks.

Common questions

Columbus parent FAQs.

Which Columbus clinic should we tour first?

If you live north or northwest — Worthington, Westerville, Dublin, Powell, Upper Arlington — tour our Worthington clinic on Wilson Bridge Rd. If you’re on the east side — Gahanna, New Albany, Bexley, Whitehall — the Gahanna clinic on Airport Dr is closer. Not sure? Call (614) 681-1030 and we’ll pick the better fit based on your schedule.

Do we need an autism diagnosis before starting ABA?

Yes — insurance requires a current autism diagnosis to authorize ABA. If your child doesn’t have one yet, we can help. Our team coordinates evaluations and most Columbus families don’t wait the typical 12–18 months that hospital systems quote.

What ages do you serve?

Ages 2 through 18. Our early intervention program (ages 2–5) is the most intensive, but we run school-age and adolescent programs at both Columbus clinics as well.

Do you take Ohio Medicaid?

Yes — including CareSource, Buckeye Health Plan, Molina Healthcare, Aetna Better Health, UnitedHealthcare Community Plan and AmeriHealth Caritas. We also accept most commercial carriers. Call (614) 681-1030 and we’ll verify your specific plan in 24–48 hours.

How long is your waitlist in Columbus?

There isn’t one. Both our Gahanna and Worthington clinics have open spots, and most families start within 72 hours of completing intake — not the multi-month wait you’ll hear about at other Central Ohio providers.

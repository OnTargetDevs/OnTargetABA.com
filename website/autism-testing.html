When to consider testing

When should you consider Autism Testing?

Many Salt Lake Valley parents contact us after noticing one â€” or several â€” of the signs below.

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, early identification of autism spectrum disorder allows children to access support sooner.

Delayed Speech

Slower-than-expected language development or fewer words than peers at the same age.

Limited eye contact

Avoiding or rarely making eye contact during play or conversation.

Not responding to name

Inconsistent response to their name, even when their hearing is unaffected.

Repetitive behaviors

Patterns of repeated movement, sound, or play that go beyond ordinary routine.

Sensory sensitivities

Strong reactions to sounds, textures, lights, or other everyday sensory input.

Social interaction difficulties

Struggles initiating play, sharing attention, or engaging back-and-forth with peers.

Getting an autism evaluation does not label your child. It provides clarity and direction.

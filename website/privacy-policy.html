State privacy rights — Utah & Ohio

Because On Target ABA operates clinics in Utah and Ohio, the privacy rights described in this section may apply to you depending on your state of residence. These rights are in addition to (not in place of) any rights you may have under federal law, including HIPAA for information collected in the course of treatment.

Utah residents

Utah Consumer Privacy Act (UCPA)

Under the Utah Consumer Privacy Act (sometimes referred to as UTDAL), Utah residents may have the right to:

  • Confirm whether we process your personal data and access that data.
  • Receive a portable copy of your personal data.
  • Delete personal data you provided to us.
  • Opt out of the sale of personal data and of targeted advertising.
Ohio residents

Ohio Personal Privacy Act (OPPA)

Pending and forthcoming Ohio legislation (including the Ohio Personal Privacy Act, OPPA) is expected to give Ohio residents rights similar to those listed for Utah. Where OPPA or a successor statute applies, Ohio residents may have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we collect about you.
  • Access, correct, or delete that information.
  • Opt out of the sale of personal data and of targeted advertising.
  • Receive notice of any material changes to how that data is used.

How to submit a request

To exercise any of the rights above, please email privacy@ontargetaba.com with the subject line "State Privacy Request" and tell us (a) your state of residence, (b) the right(s) you want to exercise, and (c) enough information for us to verify your identity. You may also call us at (888) 989-5011.

We will respond within forty-five (45) days of receipt and may extend that period by another forty-five (45) days when reasonably necessary. We will not charge a fee for the first request you make in any twelve-month period. If we deny a request, you have the right to appeal that decision by replying to our denial within sixty (60) days.

HIPAA exemption

Most state privacy statutes, including the UCPA and OPPA, exclude information that is governed by the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). Protected Health Information ("PHI") collected in connection with clinical services, billing, or care coordination is handled under our HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices and is not subject to the request mechanisms described above. If your request relates to PHI, please contact our Privacy Officer at privacy@ontargetaba.com.
